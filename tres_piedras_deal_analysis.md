## Walkthrough Checklist — Tomorrow at 2pm

### BEFORE You Walk In

Ask your agent:
- [ ] Is this lot in the 55+ restricted zone of Desert Inn Mobile Estates?
- [ ] What's the kitchen cabinet material (solid wood vs. laminate)?
- [ ] Any offers on 3132 Gavilan yet (listed 4 days ago at $255K)?
- [ ] How many other offers has the REO received? Any history of cancelled deals?
- [ ] HOA dues, rules, rental restrictions?

### Exterior

- [ ] Roof condition — any damage, loose shingles, pooling?
- [ ] Exterior paint — really is fresh? Any peeling?
- [ ] Skirting — note the damaged section at front corner
- [ ] Belly wrap — any animal entry points?
- [ ] Carport structure — stable, no rust?
- [ ] Awnings — functional?
- [ ] Landscaping — any tree roots threatening foundation?
- [ ] Utility locates (red marks on sidewalk) — ask agent what work is pending
- [ ] Sidewalk markings — what are these for?
- [ ] Water meter — accessible, no leaks visible?
- [ ] Gas meter and line — no corrosion?
- [ ] Electrical panel outside — proper, no burn marks?

### The Boarded Window

- [ ] What's the actual damage?
- [ ] Is it a break-in? Look inside for other signs of entry
- [ ] How much to replace (budgeted $500)

### HVAC (Goodman GSX130481BD, 2014)

- [ ] Does the unit fire up?
- [ ] Cold air actually cold? Hot air actually hot?
- [ ] Any rust, damage, or recent repairs visible?
- [ ] Condensate line clear, no pooling?
- [ ] Age ~11.5 years — plan for $3K concession at sale

### Floors — CRITICAL

Walk EVERY room and feel for soft spots. Bounce-test in:
- [ ] Around every toilet
- [ ] Around tubs and shower pan
- [ ] Under kitchen sink (walk in front of it)
- [ ] Dishwasher area
- [ ] Washer/dryer hookups
- [ ] Entry doors (water can wick in)
- [ ] Under window where board is (water intrusion?)

**Any soft spot = subfloor repair = $500–$2,000 each**

### Ceilings

- [ ] Water stains anywhere? Especially in hallways, near vents, around ceiling fans
- [ ] Sagging or discoloration on popcorn ceiling
- [ ] Bathroom ceilings — mold, moisture damage
- [ ] Crack in ceiling seams along beams

### Kitchen

- [ ] Cabinets solid wood or laminate? (Wood = paintable)
- [ ] Under-sink plumbing — any leaks, damage, replaced pipes?
- [ ] Drain lines — PVC or original galvanized?
- [ ] Water pressure at sink
- [ ] All drawers open, cabinet doors close properly
- [ ] Garbage disposal works?
- [ ] Exhaust fan/hood works?

### Bathrooms (both)

- [ ] Toilets flush properly, no running
- [ ] Water pressure at sinks AND tubs (low pressure = polybutylene concern)
- [ ] Tub/shower — any cracks, stains, active leaks?
- [ ] Caulk condition around tubs — black mold?
- [ ] Under vanity — any leaks, water damage, smell?
- [ ] Exhaust fans work?
- [ ] Primary bath wallpaper — condition behind it (peel a corner if safe)?

### Plumbing (Critical — 1987 Manufactured Home)

- [ ] What kind of supply lines? (Polybutylene = deal killer, replace entire system)
- [ ] Any visible repairs, patches, or mismatched pipe sections?
- [ ] Hot water heater — age on label, tank condition, any corrosion?
- [ ] Main shut-off valve — can you find it, does it turn?
- [ ] Washer hookup — corrosion on valves?

### Electrical

- [ ] Electrical panel — breakers (good) or fuses (old, needs upgrade)?
- [ ] Panel brand — if it says "Federal Pacific" or "Zinsco," budget $1,500 to replace
- [ ] Test outlets in every room (bring a phone charger or outlet tester)
- [ ] GFCIs in kitchen and baths work?
- [ ] Light switches all function?
- [ ] Ceiling fan switches work?

### Signs of Squatter/Theft Damage (Bank-Owned Risk)

- [ ] Are all appliances still there (fridge, stove, dishwasher)?
- [ ] Copper plumbing still intact (no cut pipes)?
- [ ] HVAC condenser coils intact (no cut lines)?
- [ ] Any broken locks beyond the boarded window?
- [ ] Any graffiti, trash, signs of occupation?
- [ ] Smell test — mildew, smoke, pet urine, mothballs?

### Pest Inspection

- [ ] Rodent droppings anywhere?
- [ ] Signs of termites (mud tubes, wood damage)?
- [ ] Cockroach evidence in kitchen/baths?

### Windows & Doors

- [ ] All windows open/close/lock?
- [ ] Window seals intact (look for fogging between panes)?
- [ ] Single-pane or dual-pane? (Single = budget for replacement over time)
- [ ] Front/back doors close properly, weatherstripping intact?
- [ ] Interior doors all function?

### Closet & Storage

- [ ] Guest bath closet doors — count how many need replacement
- [ ] All closet doors functional?
- [ ] Laundry area — washer/dryer hookups in good shape?

### Proof of Funds — Do It Right

- Full first page of bank statement (NOT a screenshot)
- Cross out last 4 of account number
- Show $180K–$220K range — enough to be credible, not so much you look loaded
- If you have multiple accounts, pick the right one to show
- Have this ready to submit WITH the offer

### Don't Decide at the Walkthrough

Tell your agent you want to sleep on it and submit the offer the next morning. This prevents emotional decisions and gives you time to re-run numbers after seeing the condition IRL.

## Red Flags That Would Make Me Walk Away

1. **Polybutylene supply lines confirmed** — $8K+ plumbing replacement kills the deal
2. **Multiple soft floor spots** — subfloor failure is a cascade
3. **Active water intrusion** (not just old stains) — ongoing structural risk
4. **Federal Pacific or Zinsco electrical panel** — potential fire hazard, lenders don't like
5. **Significant theft damage** — copper stripped, HVAC gutted, appliances missing
6. **Pest infestation** — especially termites in subfloor
7. **Sewer line issue** if utility locates are for that reason
8. **55+ restriction actively enforced** combined with any of the above
